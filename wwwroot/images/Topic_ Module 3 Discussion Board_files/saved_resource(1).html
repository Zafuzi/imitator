1. TCP and UDP are the two types of IP traffic. TCP is often used in scenarios where connection reliability is important, whereas UDP is used more frequently when latency is a greater issue.

Because UDP is a connectionless protocol, meaning that it can send information without connecting to the recipient or waiting for a callback, it is commonly used in VoIP calls, some gaming instances, and DNS resolving.

TDP on the other hand is a connection based protocol and must have a solid connection to each node otherwise the connection will be terminated. For this reason HTTP, FTP, and SMTP utilize TCP because it is more reliable than UDP and they are all protocols that rely on a solid connection. This also means that TCP is slower than UDP and less useful in situations that require quick communications like video calling.
